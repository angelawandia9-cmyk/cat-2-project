Our Story

How a small idea became Nairobi's favourite corner café

From a Small Kiosk to a Community Favourite

Zuri Café started in 2019 as a small coffee kiosk along Waiyaki Way, run by two friends, Angela and Amani, who simply wanted to serve honest, good coffee to the busy Nairobi crowd. What began as a four-seat stall has grown into a full sit-down café in Westlands, but our heart hasn't changed.

We still source our beans from small-scale farmers in Nyeri and Kiambu, paying fair prices and roasting in small batches every week. Every pastry on our menu is baked fresh each morning by our in-house baker, Grace, who trained at the Kenya Utalii College before joining us in 2021.

What We Stand For

Quality First

Every cup is brewed to order. No shortcuts, no pre-made batches sitting for hours.

Community Roots

We host monthly open-mic evenings and support local artists by displaying their work on our walls.

Sustainability

Compostable cups, reusable mugs for dine-in customers, and zero single-use plastic straws.
